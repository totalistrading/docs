## What is Totalis?

Totalis is the derivatives layer for prediction markets. We let you take bets from different prediction market venues, stack them together, and build custom structured positions like parlays.

Our first product is **custom parlays on prediction markets**. You pick outcomes across multiple markets, combine them into a single leveraged bet, and market makers compete to give you the best payout odds. All trades are facilitated through our infrastructure — a request-for-quote (RFQ) system for price discovery and non-custodial Solana escrow for settlement.

We currently support Kalshi as a market venue, with Polymarket and other prediction market platforms coming soon.

## Can't I just do this on Kalshi?

Kalshi only offers combos on specific sports markets with very limited options. This is because Kalshi has to create an orderbook for every single combination of their combos, which is highly inefficient.

Totalis takes a different approach — we sit on top of prediction markets as a derivatives layer and use an RFQ system to price any combination of outcomes. This means you can construct parlays on markets across sports, crypto, weather, entertainment, and economic events, and eventually combine bets across multiple venues in a single parlay.

## What markets does Totalis offer?

We currently offer markets that:

- Are recurring

- Have reliable liquidity on Kalshi

- Settle within one week

These include structured combinations across sports, macro, politics, crypto, and weather events.

For a detailed and up-to-date list of supported markets, users can query our public API endpoint (https://docs.totalis.trade/api-reference/markets/list).

Our focus is on high-frequency, short-duration markets that enable continuous structured positioning and rapid capital turnover.

## What chain are you on?

Totalis runs on **Solana**. All escrow accounts, deposits, and settlements are executed on-chain via our `parlay_escrow` Anchor program. We currently operate on Solana devnet, with mainnet deployment coming soon.

## How secure is it?

All funds are held in a **Solana escrow program** — not by Totalis. When a trade is matched, both the user's stake and the market maker's risk are deposited into an on-chain escrow PDA (Program Derived Address). Neither party can withdraw unilaterally; only the settlement authority (our oracle) can release funds, and only based on the verified outcome from Kalshi.

Users can optionally grant permission for the server to help sign transactions for smoother UX. We use secure embedded wallets (Privy TEE) where private keys never leave the Trusted Execution Environment.

Collateral is locked only for the duration of the position and settles automatically upon resolution. Every escrow has a timeout — if markets don't resolve in time, funds are automatically refunded to both parties.

For full details, see the [Payment Architecture guide](/guides/payment-architecture).

## What happens if a market gets cancelled on Kalshi?

If a Kalshi market is cancelled or delisted before all legs in a parlay have resolved, the escrow's timeout protection kicks in. The timeout service automatically cancels the escrow after the deadline (default 1 hour) and refunds both the user's stake and the market maker's risk to their respective wallets. No party loses funds due to an external market cancellation.

## Will you add markets from other prediction markets like Polymarket? Can I make cross-platform parlays?

Yes. Totalis is built as a venue-agnostic derivatives layer — our infrastructure is designed to aggregate markets across prediction market platforms. Polymarket support is coming soon, and the goal is to let you combine outcomes from any supported venue into a single parlay. Cross-platform parlays (e.g., a Kalshi leg + a Polymarket leg in the same bet) are on the roadmap.

## Are you an exchange?

Totalis is a derivatives layer that sits on top of prediction markets. We facilitate peer-to-peer structured transactions between users and market makers, settled on-chain via Solana escrow. We don't operate a traditional central limit order book or list primary markets — we aggregate outcomes from existing venues and enable new structured products like parlays on top of them.

## How does Totalis make money?

We take a **1% fee on the profit** earned by the winning side of each trade.

For example, if you bet 100 at 4x odds and all legs hit, your total payout is 400. Your profit is 300, so Totalis takes 3 (1% of 300). You receive 397.
