The Partner Console is where your team manages your live integration — without filing a ticket. It's
read/write for your own partner data only; everything you see is scoped to your partner account.

## Access

Console access is **invite-based**. A Totalis operator invites a teammate by email and shares the
console URL. There's no password: you sign in with a **one-time code** sent to your email.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Get invited">
    Ask your Totalis contact to invite your work email. Each member is invited individually; the
    email address is the identity — sign in with the exact address that was invited.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Sign in">
    Open the console and enter your email. You'll receive a one-time code; enter it to log in. No
    wallet, no password.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Note>
  If sign-in succeeds but you can't get in, your email probably hasn't been invited yet (or was
  invited with different casing/address). Ask your Totalis contact to confirm the invite.
</Note>

## API keys

The console lists the keys issued to you — one per user who completed
[OAuth onboarding](/partners/onboarding). For each key you see its prefix, the user it belongs to,
its scopes, status, last-used time, and request count. Secrets are never shown (they're only
returned once, at the token exchange).

**Revoking** a key takes effect immediately and can't be undone — the user has to re-authorize to
restore access. Use it if a key is compromised or a user offboards.

<Info>
  You can't **mint** keys from the console — keys are created through the OAuth consent flow so every
  key is tied to a consenting user. To issue a key, onboard the user.
</Info>

## Webhooks

From the Webhooks page you can:

- Set your **endpoint URL** (HTTPS) and choose which [events](/partners/webhooks#event-catalog) to receive.
- **Rotate your signing secret** — shown once; the old one stops working immediately, so update your
  verifier in lockstep.
- Browse **recent deliveries** and **replay** a delivered or dead-lettered one after fixing an issue
  on your side.

See [Webhooks](/partners/webhooks) for the signature scheme and verification code.

## Roles

Invited members have a role (`admin` or `member`). Roles are recorded on the membership currently; as
console capabilities grow, higher-risk actions will be gated to `admin`. Invite teammates with the
least privilege they need.
