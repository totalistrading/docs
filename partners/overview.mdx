The Totalis Partner Platform lets you embed Totalis trading into your own product. You onboard your
users through a hosted OAuth flow, receive a **scoped, per-user access token**, call the Totalis API
on their behalf, and get **signed webhooks** when things happen to their positions and balances.

## Core concepts

- **Partner** — your registered application, identified by a `client_id` and authenticated with a
  `client_secret`. A Totalis operator creates this and sets your **scope ceiling** (the maximum your
  users can ever grant you) and your withdrawal caps.
- **Scopes** — fine-grained permissions (e.g. `positions:read`, `trading:write`). Every access token
  carries a set of scopes; each API endpoint and webhook event requires specific ones. See the
  [scope reference](#scopes) below.
- **Access token** — a per-user credential (`api_live_…`) minted when a user completes onboarding.
  It authenticates every API call you make *on that user's behalf* and is constrained to the scopes
  they approved. It expires; you refresh it with a refresh token.
- **Grant** — the standing relationship between your partner app and one user. Revoking a grant (by
  you or the user) invalidates all its tokens at once.

## Base URL & authentication

All partner APIs are served from:

```
https://api.totalis.trade
```

Authenticate API calls on behalf of a user by sending their access token as a bearer token:

```bash
curl https://api.totalis.trade/rfqs \
  -H "Authorization: Bearer api_live_xxxxxxxxxxxx"
```

<Note>
  Because a partner access token is an API key under the hood, you may also send it as
  `X-API-Key: api_live_…`. Both are accepted on every authenticated endpoint. Use whichever fits
  your HTTP client.
</Note>

The OAuth endpoints (`/oauth/token`, `/oauth/revoke`) authenticate **your app** instead — with your
`client_id` / `client_secret` — and are covered in [Onboarding](/partners/onboarding).

## Scopes

A token can only do what its scopes allow, and you can never request more than your partner's
ceiling. Users may further narrow scopes on the consent screen.

| Scope | Grants | Risk |
| --- | --- | --- |
| `account:read` | View account profile and username. | low |
| `markets:read` | Browse markets and exclusion groups. | low |
| `positions:read` | View quote requests, portfolio, stats, and P&L. | low |
| `balances:read` | View wallet and vault balances and delegation status. | low |
| `trading:write` | Create, modify, commit, and cancel quote requests (place parlays). | medium |
| `funds:deposit` | Move USDC into the vault. | low |
| `funds:withdraw` | Move USDC out of the vault/wallet to any address. **Can drain the account.** | high |
| `account:write` | Change username and profile settings. | medium |
| `tx:sign` | Submit arbitrary signed Solana transactions. | high |
| `keys:manage` | Create, list, and revoke API keys. | high |
| `mm:quote` | Read RFQs and submit/confirm quotes as a market maker. | medium |

<Warning>
  The **high-risk** scopes (`funds:withdraw`, `tx:sign`, `keys:manage`) are excluded from default
  grants and require explicit opt-in by both Totalis (your ceiling) and the user (consent). Request
  only the scopes you actually use — narrower grants convert better and reduce your risk surface.
</Warning>

## Getting started

<Steps>
  <Step title="Get registered">
    Contact Totalis to register your app. You'll receive a `client_id` and a `client_secret`, and
    you'll provide your redirect URI(s) and the scope ceiling you need.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Onboard a user">
    Send a user through the [OAuth flow](/partners/onboarding) and exchange the authorization code
    for an access token.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Call the API">
    Use the access token as a bearer token against `https://api.totalis.trade`. See the
    [API Reference](/api-reference/introduction).
  </Step>
  <Step title="Subscribe to webhooks">
    Configure your endpoint and events in the [Partner Console](/partners/console), then
    [verify signatures](/partners/webhooks#verifying-signatures) on every delivery.
  </Step>
</Steps>
